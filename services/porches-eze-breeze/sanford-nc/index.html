Soil Conditions

Deep River basin soils — a mix of Piedmont clay and sandy loam. Carolina Trace and lakefront properties sit on better-draining sandy clay, while the western side of Sanford has denser clay requiring deeper footings. HOA Notes

Porches & Eze Breeze Windows in Sanford FAQs

Can you renovate an existing screened porch on an older Sanford home?
Yes — porch renovation is one of our most common Sanford projects. Older screened porches often have rotting floor joists, torn or sagging screens, deteriorating roofing, and outdated railing. We assess the existing structure to determine what can be salvaged and what needs replacement. In many cases, the foundation and main posts are solid, and we can rebuild the floor, replace the roof, re-screen, and add Eze Breeze panels without a complete demolition and rebuild. This approach typically saves 30–40% compared to a full new build.
What porch design maximizes Carolina Trace lake views?
We orient the porch's longest side toward the lake and use floor-to-ceiling Eze Breeze panels (no knee wall) on the lake-facing openings. This creates an unobstructed view from any seated position. The side walls can be partial-height with screens or panels above for privacy from neighboring properties. A vaulted or shed-roof ceiling on the lake side adds visual height and prevents the porch from feeling like a tunnel. We position the primary seating area facing the water so the view is the focal point.
How much does porch construction cost in Sanford?
Sanford porch projects typically cost 5–10% less than equivalent builds in Cary or Apex due to lower permit fees, fewer HOA constraints, and the efficiency of working on larger, easier-access lots. A basic screened porch (200 sq ft) runs $18,000–$30,000. A premium build with Eze Breeze panels, finished ceiling, and electrical runs $30,000–$55,000. Carolina Trace lakefront porches with premium finishes and view-optimized design are at the higher end.
Does Lee County permitting differ from Wake County for porches?
Lee County permits are processed faster (5–10 business days versus 10–14 in Wake County) and fees are lower. The structural requirements are the same — NC building code applies statewide. The main difference is responsiveness: Lee County inspectors are generally easier to schedule and more flexible on timing. This can shave 1–2 weeks off the project timeline compared to Wake County. We manage all Lee County permitting for Sanford projects.
