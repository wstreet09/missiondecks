Porches & Eze Breeze Windows in Fuquay-Varina FAQs

Is it cheaper to build a screened porch now or convert the builder deck later?
Building a screened porch from scratch during or immediately after new construction is typically 15–20% cheaper than converting an existing deck later. The reason: a new-build porch is designed as an integrated structure with footings, framing, and roof sized for the full load from day one. A conversion requires evaluating the existing deck, adding reinforcement, and working around existing conditions — all of which add cost and complexity. If you know you want a screened porch, plan it early.
What porch size works for a FV family with young children?
For families with kids, we recommend a minimum of 14x16 feet (224 sq ft) to accommodate a dining table, a play area, and traffic flow without feeling cramped. This gives you room for a small outdoor rug, a toy bin, and adult seating without everything being on top of each other. FV lot sizes easily accommodate this footprint. Larger families or those who entertain frequently should consider 16x20 (320 sq ft) for more flexible zoning.
How do Eze Breeze panels handle FV summer heat?
Eze Breeze panels are vinyl — they allow solar heat gain when closed, so on full-sun summer days, a closed Eze Breeze porch can get warm. The solution is twofold: keep panels open for ventilation during mild days (they slide up like windows), and install two ceiling fans for air movement on hot days. Tinted Eze Breeze panels reduce heat gain by roughly 30% compared to clear panels. For FV's hottest months (July–August), fans plus open panels provide comfortable conditions; panels close when rain threatens.
Does a screened porch need a permit in Fuquay-Varina?
Yes — any roofed structure attached to a home requires a Wake County building permit. The permit covers structural adequacy, roofline attachment, and electrical work. Processing takes 1–2 weeks. If your FV property is within a Bentwinds or other HOA, architectural review adds 2–4 weeks. We manage all permitting and HOA submissions as part of the project scope — you do not need to visit any government office.
