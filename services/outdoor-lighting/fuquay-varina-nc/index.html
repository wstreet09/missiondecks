New-construction FV homes come with one builder-installed floodlight above the back door — and that is usually it. A single harsh floodlight turns your backyard into a washed-out spotlight that is neither safe (deep shadows beyond the light cone) nor inviting. Local Tip

If your FV home faces west, your backyard gets the best sunset light — but by 8 PM in summer it is completely dark. Set your outdoor lighting timer to click on 15 minutes before sunset so the transition from daylight to artificial light is gradual and seamless. Outdoor Lighting in Fuquay-Varina FAQs

What lighting should I add first to a new FV home?
Start with safety: step lights on any deck stairs and post cap lights marking the deck perimeter. Add path lights along the walkway between the back door and the deck or patio. This foundational layer costs $1,500–$2,500 and immediately makes your backyard safe to navigate at night. Once the safety layer is in, add ambiance: string lights over the dining area and 2–3 uplights on landscape features. This completes the system for $3,500–$5,000 total.
How do you prevent light from bothering FV neighbors on close lots?
We use shielded, downward-directed fixtures that focus light on your property without spilling onto neighboring yards. Low-voltage LEDs produce controlled beams (unlike floodlights that spray light in all directions). Path lights with top shields direct light downward onto the path surface. Uplights aimed at trees or walls keep the light within your property line. We verify light coverage during the evening adjustment visit to ensure zero trespass onto neighboring lots.
Can you install permanent string light poles in my FV backyard?
Yes — this is one of our most popular FV installations. We set 10–12 foot steel or cedar posts in concrete footings and run commercial-grade string lights between them at 8–9 feet overhead. The posts are positioned to frame the dining or seating area, and the lights are permanently mounted (no more hanging and taking down seasonal lights). This setup costs $800–$1,500 depending on the number of posts and run length, and it creates the evening ambiance that most FV homeowners are looking for.
Do FV outdoor lights work with smart home systems?
Yes. Low-voltage transformers can be connected to WiFi-enabled timers that integrate with Alexa, Google Home, or Apple HomeKit. You can set schedules, adjust brightness zones, and turn lights on or off from your phone. We install the transformer and timer hardware and walk you through the smart home connection. Most systems pair in under 10 minutes.
