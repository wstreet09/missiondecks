Custom Decks in Fuquay-Varina FAQs

Why are so many Fuquay-Varina homeowners replacing builder-grade decks?
FV has grown rapidly since 2010, and most new construction includes a basic 12x12 or 12x14 pressure-treated deck that meets code minimums. These decks use standard-grade lumber, basic railings, and minimal features. Within 2–3 years, homeowners realize the space is too small for their family's needs and the materials are showing wear from NC humidity. A custom replacement typically doubles the usable area and upgrades to composite materials that last 25–50 years with minimal maintenance.
What are the Bentwinds HOA requirements for deck construction?
Bentwinds is a gated golf community with detailed architectural guidelines. Deck submissions require scaled drawings, material specifications with color samples, and a site plan showing the structure relative to property lines and the golf course. The review committee meets monthly, so plan for a 30–45 day approval cycle. Bentwinds generally prefers earth-toned materials that blend with the natural landscape. We prepare complete submission packages and have a track record of first-pass approvals in Bentwinds.
How do soil conditions differ across Fuquay-Varina neighborhoods?
Northern FV (Crooked Creek, Bentwinds) sits on Piedmont clay ridges similar to Holly Springs — dense, expansive clay that requires 18–24 inch footings. Southern FV (Sunset Bluffs, Holland Hills) has more variable soil with sandy clay patches that drain better but can settle unevenly. We assess soil conditions at every FV site during the design consultation, and we adjust footing depth and diameter based on what we find on your specific lot.
Is Fuquay-Varina growing too fast for contractors to keep up?
FV's growth means high demand for outdoor construction, and scheduling can be competitive during peak spring and summer months. We recommend starting your design consultation in winter (January–February) to lock in a spring build date. Our Holly Springs proximity means our travel time to FV is minimal — we are in Fuquay-Varina neighborhoods daily and can often schedule projects faster than Raleigh-based contractors who have longer logistics to manage.
